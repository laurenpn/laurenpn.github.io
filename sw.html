The Seiberg-Witten Invariants (Fall 2023)

About:

The goal of this study group is to learn about the Seiberg-Witten invariants for smooth 4-manifolds. The study group will run in parallel to math 8360, "Topics in Topology," taught by Prof. Anar Akhmedov. We will try to organize the study group so as to complement the material covered in the math 8360 lectures.

Tentative plan: Following our primary reference [M], we will introduce the necessary background required to state the Seiberg-Witten equations; then we will then study the moduli space of solutions to these equations, and define the associated invariants for smooth 4-manifolds. We will then touch on some notable results obtained using these invariants, such as Donaldson's theorem, the Thom conjecture, Fintushel-Stern knot surgery, applications to scalar curvature, etc.

With the remaining time, we will survey some recent developments in 4-manifold topology related to Seiberg-Witten invariants and gauge theory, determined by participant interests. We may also attempt to understand the origins of the Seiberg-Witten equations in physics, coming from $\mathcal{N}=2$ supersymmetric gauge theory.
